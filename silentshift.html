My work explores the relationship
between femininity, discomfort, and
societal expectation through the
symbolic use of shoes. Shoes often
reflect both physical and emotional
experience—they can provide
comfort, but they can also represent
restriction and sacrifice. In many
cases, women are expected to wear
uncomfortable shoes as a way to
embody ideals of femininity, beauty,
and presentation.


The concept of “shifting” becomes
central within this work. Physically,
when a person stands in discomfort
for too long, they instinctively shift
their weight or reposition
themselves in search of relief. This
movement is subtle and often
unconscious.

Conceptually, shifting also reflects the
ways women are frequently expected
to adjust themselves for others—
emotionally, socially, and culturally.
These acts of adaptation can become
so normalized that they are performed
without question or even awareness.


Through graphic design, I use visual
symbolism and composition to
examine how discomfort can become
embedded within everyday
experiences and expectations. My
work invites viewers to reflect on the
quiet, automatic ways people—
particularly women—alter themselves
to fit societal standards, and how
these shifts can reveal larger
conversations surrounding identity,
pressure, and gender roles.

Silent Shift


Silent Shift is a board game where players
navigate a womans life’s challenges
through high-heel avatars, each
representing different levels of difficulty.
Inspired by real experiences faced by
women, the game blends humor and
empathy as players encounter both
setbacks and empowering moments.
Through dice rolls, card draws, and event
spaces, the game highlights how everyday
obstacles and small victories shape one’s
path. Visually, Silent Shift is colorful and
feminine, with a stylized path and card
icons inspired by fashion motifs. It invites
players to step into both the fun and
frustration of walking through life in
someone else’s heels.
